Why therapy?

 People seek psychological therapy and emotional support for a number of reasons, including stress, anxiety, trauma, loss, feeling stuck, experiencing work or relationship issues, as well as the desire for personal growth and a greater sense of well-being.

My approach.

  In my practice I adopt an integrative approach, drawing on a range of psychological models to ensure the therapy fits the specific needs and preference of the people I work with. I have a particular interest in Acceptance and Commitment Therapy as well as Mindfulness, but will incorporate the therapeutic techniques from Cognitive Behavioural Therapy, systemic therapy, brief solution focused therapy, and motivational interviewing, when deemed appropriate.

Choose professional.

  As a Clinical Psychologist, I am trained in a range of therapeutic models to work with people that are experiencing all types of behavioural, emotional and/or mental health difficulties. In my practice I offer psychological therapy to young people and adults affected by anxiety, stress, OCD, trauma, depression and chronic pain. I have experience of working with people affected by orofacial pain conditions, such as nerve injuries and temporomandibular dysfunction as well as using CBT to help clients overcome specific phobias, including fear of needles and injections.
